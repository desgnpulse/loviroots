# Loviroots — Product Taxonomy & Website Structure (Draft for Review)

**Purpose:** Organize everything Loviroots currently makes — and everything implied but not yet named — into a structure that can drive the website's navigation and product pages. Items marked **[CONFIRM]** are things I inferred from "you get the drift" / "other hair oil" / "different types" / "etc." — the principal should say keep, kill, or rename.

---

## 1. Proposed Website Top-Level Navigation

1. Hair Care
2. Skin Care
3. Pain & Joint Relief (Wellness)
4. Pure & Cold-Pressed Oils
5. Essential Oils
6. Traditional Herbs & Wild Botanicals (the "apothecary" layer)
7. Our Heritage / The Knowledge (story content, not a shop category)

This separates **what people are shopping for** (relief, hair growth, skin) from **what it's made of** (raw oils, herbs, barks) — most natural-product brands need both views, because a customer arrives either with a problem ("my joints hurt") or with an ingredient in mind ("I want castor oil").

## 8. Our Heritage / The Knowledge (Content Layer — Not a Product Category)

This generational knowledge is your biggest brand asset and currently has nowhere to live structurally. Recommend treating it as content tied to products rather than a separate shop section:

- A short "traditional use" note on every product page (e.g., what Comfort Oil's herb blend has historically been used for, where the practice comes from)
- A standalone "Our Heritage" page telling the bigger story (generational knowledge, wildcrafting, where ingredients are sourced)
- Optionally a "Learn" or "Apothecary Notes" section if there's enough material per-herb to justify individual pages
