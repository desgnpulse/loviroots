Castor oil is the most recommended product for 4C hair growth online. The number of posts and videos claiming it grows hair is vast. Most of what you have read about why it works is incomplete.

Here is what the science actually supports, and what it does not.

---

## What castor oil cannot do

Castor oil cannot create new hair follicles. Nothing applied topically can.

Hair growth happens inside the follicle, which sits in the dermis beneath the skin surface. Topical oils do not penetrate to the dermis. They work on the scalp surface and the hair shaft, not on the growth mechanism itself.

Any product claiming to regrow hair through topical application alone is overstating what is possible.

---

## What castor oil can do

Scalp circulation is connected to follicle health. Better blood flow means better nutrient delivery to active follicles.

The ricinoleic acid in castor oil has vasodilatory properties. It may slightly increase local circulation when massaged in. This is not dramatic. Over consistent daily application, it contributes to follicle support.

Castor oil is also a strong occlusive. On the hair shaft, it reduces moisture loss along the length of the strand. 4C hair is structurally more prone to moisture loss than straighter types. The tight coil pattern creates more surface area and more exposure to dryness at each bend point.

Sealing moisture into the shaft is where castor oil has the clearest, most documented effect.

---

## The black castor oil distinction

Jamaican black castor oil is made by roasting the seeds before pressing. Roasting produces ash. That ash, combined with oil during pressing, raises the pH slightly.

A higher-pH product opens the hair cuticle, which in theory allows better penetration. In practice, the difference between black castor oil and cold-pressed castor oil for scalp use is modest. Both contain the same primary fatty acid. The roasting changes texture and colour. It does not fundamentally change the mechanism.

---

## What actually drives retention

Retention is the more relevant metric for most people. Growing hair matters less than keeping the hair you already grow.

4C hair breaks at weak points. Those points are dry sections, manipulation, and mechanical stress. Shrinkage means length is invisible, which leads to over-handling.

Castor oil contributes to retention by sealing moisture on the shaft and reducing breakage at manipulation points. It is a good occlusive that addresses one main cause of length loss.

Consistency matters more than which version of castor oil you buy.

---

## The honest recommendation

Use castor oil on the scalp and lengths. Massage it in. Let it sit before washing. Do it regularly.

Combine it with a moisture-sealing routine. Shea butter and castor oil together cover both emollient and occlusive functions. Neither one is more important than the other.

Manage expectations. Most people see improved retention and texture within eight to twelve weeks. Reduced breakage and better moisture balance, not overnight length.
