Three products with castor oil on the label. Three very different things.

Pure castor oil. Black castor oil. Comfort Oil. Customers regularly use these interchangeably. They are not the same product and are not meant for the same purpose.

---

## Pure castor oil (cold-pressed)

Cold-pressed castor oil is extracted from castor seeds without heat or solvents. The process preserves the full ricinoleic acid content and the minor fatty acids and plant compounds that survive alongside it.

The result is a pale yellow, viscous oil with a mild earthy smell. It has the highest functional purity of the three.

**Best for:** scalp application, joint massage, skin barrier support, and use as an occlusive sealant on the hair shaft. The base for anything requiring maximum ricinoleic acid concentration.

---

## Black castor oil (Jamaican-style)

Black castor oil starts with roasted castor seeds. The seeds are charred before pressing. The ash produced during roasting remains in the oil, which changes two things: pH and colour.

The pH increase slightly opens the hair cuticle. In theory, this allows better penetration of the oil into the hair shaft. In practice, the difference over cold-pressed castor oil is modest.

The colour is dark brown to black. The smell is stronger, with a characteristic burnt note. Some people strongly prefer it for hair use on sensory grounds alone.

**Best for:** 4C hair, scalp massage, hot oil treatments. The performance difference over plain cold-pressed castor oil is small. The preference is often personal, not performance-based.

---

## Comfort Oil (herb-infused)

Comfort Oil is not a straight castor oil. It is a blend.

Cold-pressed castor oil forms the base. Eucalyptus and ginger root are slow-infused into the oil at low heat over an extended period. The result is a warming, darker-coloured oil with a distinct herbal scent.

The ricinoleic acid from the castor base is present. Eucalyptol from the eucalyptus and gingerols from the ginger add further anti-inflammatory and analgesic compounds.

**Best for:** joint pain, muscle soreness, and morning stiffness. Not designed for hair or scalp use. The herbal compounds useful for joints are not needed on the scalp. The scent is also stronger than most people want in their hair.

---

## Which one to buy

**For hair:** pure cold-pressed or black castor oil. Start with pure cold-pressed unless you already prefer the texture and scent of the black version.

**For scalp inflammation:** pure cold-pressed castor oil. Higher ricinoleic acid concentration without the pH effect of the roasting process.

**For joint and muscle pain:** Comfort Oil. The herb infusion is what you are paying for. Plain castor oil for joint pain works to a degree, but Comfort Oil is formulated specifically for that purpose.

**For skin:** pure cold-pressed castor oil, used sparingly and always layered with a lighter product. It is too occlusive to use alone on the face.
