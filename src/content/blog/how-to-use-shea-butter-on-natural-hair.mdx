4C hair loses moisture faster than most hair types. The tight curl pattern makes it harder for the scalp's natural oils to travel down the hair shaft. By the time those oils reach the ends, the hair is already dry.

The LOC method exists to solve this. Shea butter is one of the best products for the final step.

---

## What LOC means

LOC stands for Liquid, Oil, Cream. The idea is layering:

1. **Liquid:** water or a water-based leave-in conditioner. This is the moisture.
2. **Oil:** a lightweight oil like jojoba or argan. This helps the moisture absorb.
3. **Cream:** a butter or thick cream. This seals everything in.

Shea butter goes in the third slot. Its job is to slow moisture loss, not add it. It holds in what is already there.

---

## How to apply it

Start with freshly washed or dampened hair. Work in sections.

Spray or apply your leave-in first. While the hair is still slightly damp, add a small amount of oil and work it through. Then take a pea-sized amount of shea butter. It goes further than you expect. Warm it between your palms until it melts, then press it gently into each section.

Focus on the ends, which are the oldest and driest part of the hair.

---

## Common mistakes

**Using too much.** Shea butter is dense. A small amount covers a lot of hair. Too much will leave the hair feeling heavy and waxy.

**Applying to dry hair.** Shea butter seals. If there is no moisture in the hair when you apply it, you are sealing in dryness.

**Skipping the oil layer.** Without the oil step, the shea can sit on top of the hair rather than integrating with the leave-in. The oil helps the layers bond.

---

## How often

For most 4C hair, two to three times a week is enough during the dry season. In humid months, once a week or as needed. Let your hair tell you. If it feels dry and brittle, seal more often.

---

## One jar, two uses

The same shea butter you use on your skin works for your hair. There is no special "hair version." Pure, unrefined shea butter is the same product either way.

That is the point of a one-ingredient product. It does not need a different formulation for every surface.
