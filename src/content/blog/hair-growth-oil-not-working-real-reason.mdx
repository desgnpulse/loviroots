The oil is almost never the problem.

When a hair oil stops working, or never seemed to work, the cause is usually one of four things. The oil is fine. The application is fine. Something else in the routine is undermining it.

---

## The moisture-seal sequence

Oil works as a sealant. It locks in moisture already present on the hair shaft.

If you apply oil to dry hair, it seals in dryness. The strand feels coated but is not moisturised. This is the most common reason oils seem ineffective.

The correct sequence: water-based product first, then oil. The water-based product provides moisture. The oil seals it. In most routines this means a leave-in conditioner or water mist, then oil applied while the hair is still damp.

If your hair feels greasy but dry an hour after oiling, this is the reason.

---

## Protein-moisture balance

4C hair can shift toward protein excess or moisture excess. Both present as brittle, uncooperative hair.

Protein excess feels stiff, almost crunchy. Hair breaks easily. Adding more oil does not help because the problem is structural, not a moisture issue.

Moisture excess feels limp and weak. Hair stretches more than normal before snapping. This is also not an oil problem.

If your hair responds poorly to everything you apply, stop all products for a few days. Identify which imbalance you are dealing with before adding more.

---

## Scalp buildup

Oil used repeatedly without adequate cleansing accumulates at the follicle opening. Castor oil in particular is thick. Applied daily without regular washing, it contributes to buildup faster than lighter oils.

A congested scalp slows growth rates. The follicle is impeded.

The fix is simple: wash more often. Weekly is a reasonable baseline for most people who oil regularly. Every ten to fourteen days leads to buildup in most scalp types.

---

## Length expectations

4C hair grows at roughly the same rate as other hair types. The average is 1.25 centimetres per month. In twelve months, that is 15 centimetres of new growth.

Most of that growth is not visible. Shrinkage hides it. Breakage removes it before it can be measured.

When a growth oil seems not to be working, the hair is usually growing but breaking at a similar rate. Address breakage first.

---

## What to check before changing your oil

In order of likelihood: are you applying to dry hair? Do you wash often enough? Is your protein-moisture balance off? Are you over-manipulating?

The oil is rarely the variable that needs to change.
