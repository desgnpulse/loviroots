The frustrating truth about 4C hair is that the dryness is structural. It is not about how often you moisturise. It is not about using the wrong products. It is about what the curl pattern itself does to moisture.

Here is the mechanism.

---

## Why 4C hair loses moisture faster

Sebum - the oil your scalp produces naturally - travels down the hair shaft from the follicle. In straight and wavy hair it reaches the ends within hours. In 4C hair, the tight coil pattern interrupts that journey. By the time sebum travels around each bend in the coil, the ends are already dry.

This is not a product failure. It is physics.

4C hair also has a larger surface area relative to its diameter than any other hair type. More surface area means more places where moisture can escape.

---

## The second problem: high porosity

Many people with 4C hair also have high porosity hair. The cuticle - the outer protective layer of each strand - has gaps. These gaps let moisture in quickly. They also let it escape just as quickly.

High porosity hair feels dry within hours of moisturising because the water leaves as fast as it entered.

---

## What sealing does

A sealant is a product that sits on the outside of the hair shaft and slows moisture loss. It doesn't add moisture. It holds in moisture that is already there.

This is why the LOC method works for 4C hair. You add water or a water-based leave-in first. Then a lightweight oil to help the moisture absorb. Then a butter or cream to slow evaporation.

Shea butter is one of the most effective sealants for 4C hair. It is dense enough to form a real barrier on the strand without causing heavy buildup within a few days.

---

## What doesn't work

Moisturising with only a butter. Butters seal. They don't add water. Applying shea to dry hair seals in dryness.

Applying too much. 4C hair doesn't need large amounts of product. A small amount, warmed and pressed through sections, is enough. Too much product builds up, attracts dust, and eventually blocks the cuticle.

Washing too infrequently. Buildup from product and hard water eventually coats the cuticle and prevents moisture from entering at all. A consistent wash day practice matters as much as what you apply between washes.

---

## The climate factor for Kenyan hair

Nairobi's highland climate is drier than coastal East Africa. Hair moisture evaporates faster here than in humid Mombasa. In the highlands, sealing after every moisture application matters more. In humid coastal areas, lighter products and less frequent sealing may be enough.

Let the hair tell you. If the hair feels dry within a day, the seal layer needs more or the oil step is being skipped.

---

The dryness is not a product problem. It is a structural reality that requires a structural solution. Layer in the right order, seal what you have put in, and the hair retains moisture.
