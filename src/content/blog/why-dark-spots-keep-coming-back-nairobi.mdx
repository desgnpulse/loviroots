Nairobi sits 1,795 metres above sea level. The UV index runs high year-round. It measures higher than most European cities, even on overcast days.

Dark skin doesn't show sunburn the way lighter skin does. That doesn't mean UV damage isn't happening.

---

## Why the cycle repeats

Skin inflames from a breakout, friction, or a small wound. As it heals, it deposits extra melanin at that site. Dermatologists call this post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation, or PIH. It is more pronounced in darker skin tones. The melanin response is already primed. Any insult amplifies it.

Three things drive that cycle in Nairobi.

**UV exposure.** Equatorial UV is consistent and intense throughout the year. A spot fades slowly. You go out without sun protection. It darkens again within days. The cycle runs every few weeks.

**Acne.** Each breakout leaves a mark. You clear the acne. The dark spot stays for weeks. Another appears nearby. Over months, the result is uneven tone rather than isolated marks.

**Hormonal shifts.** Pregnancy, breastfeeding, and hormonal contraceptives raise oestrogen. Elevated oestrogen stimulates melanin production. The result - patchy darkening on the cheeks, forehead, and upper lip - is called melasma. It affects a large number of Kenyan women of reproductive age and is rarely discussed clearly.

---

## What actually breaks the cycle

SPF. Not for aesthetics. For cutting the feedback loop. Apply a broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher every morning. This prevents UV from redarkening spots that are trying to fade.

Barrier repair matters alongside it. Skin that is consistently supported has fewer micro-injuries, fewer surface breakouts, and a less reactive melanin response. This is slower work. It produces durable results.

Unrefined shea butter doesn't bleach. It doesn't act on melanin directly. It rebuilds barrier function, which reduces the inflammation that starts the cycle.

---

## What doesn't break it

Brightening ingredients without SPF. Niacinamide, vitamin C, and kojic acid reduce pigmentation - but UV reverses that progress in days. Sun protection has to come first or results won't hold.

Lightening creams. Mercury and hydroquinone suppress melanin production while you use them. When you stop, the skin often produces more melanin than before. The cycle restarts, worse.

---

Dark spots are not a skin type problem. They are a feedback loop. Address the triggers and the skin corrects itself over time.
