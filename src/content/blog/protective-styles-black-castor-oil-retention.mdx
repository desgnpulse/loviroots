A protective style protects nothing if the scalp and edges are neglected during the weeks it is installed.

This is what most discussions of protective styling skip. The style is in. The hair is tucked away. Many people then ignore their scalp for four to six weeks. The hair grows, breaks at the new growth junction, and retention is lost.

---

## What retention actually means

Retention is the difference between hair growing and length increasing.

4C hair grows at a normal rate. Most people with 4C hair who see no length gain are actually growing hair. They are not retaining it. It breaks before it can be measured.

A protective style gives new growth somewhere to go without daily manipulation. The goal is only achieved if the new growth survives long enough to be retained.

---

## Where castor oil fits

The scalp continues to produce sebum inside any protective style. That is not the problem.

The problem is that sebum does not travel the full length of 4C hair. The coiled structure prevents even distribution. While the scalp may be adequately moisturised, the mid-lengths and ends inside the style can dry out.

Castor oil, diluted with a lighter carrier oil, supplements what sebum cannot reach. Apply along the edges and any accessible parts of the hair. It is thick enough to stay in place and slow moisture loss over several days.

---

## The edge problem

Edges are the most vulnerable part of the hair during protective styles. Braids, weaves, and extensions all place tension at the hairline.

That tension, especially from tight installation, restricts blood flow to the follicle. Repeated restriction causes traction alopecia. This is not reversible with oil.

Castor oil on the edges supports scalp condition around the follicle. The tension reduction has to come from the installation itself.

Edges that have been thin for over a year and not responded to any product may have a dormant follicle. That needs a dermatologist, not more oil.

---

## What to do during the style

Every three to four days: apply a diluted oil blend to the scalp through the parts of the style. Castor oil mixed with a lighter oil - jojoba or sunflower - works better than castor oil alone. Pure castor oil is too thick to distribute easily.

Every week: mist accessible lengths with water, then seal with a small amount of oil.

When taking down: deep condition before detangling. The new growth at the root will be dry and fragile. Give it moisture before putting any tension on it.

---

## The installation decisions that matter most

How tight the installation is matters more than which oil you use.

Styles installed with heavy extensions on fine 4C hair create tension that no oil can offset. Lighter extensions, scalp-friendly installation, and four to six weeks maximum will do more for retention than any topical product.

The oil supports the process. The installation decisions set the ceiling on what is possible.
