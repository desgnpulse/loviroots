Natural skin butters feel greasy when applied incorrectly. This is one of the most common reasons people stop using them. The product is usually fine. The application is usually wrong.

Here is what to change.

---

## The moisture-seal principle

Shea butter and skin butters are occlusives. They work by slowing moisture loss from the skin surface.

The mistake is applying an occlusive to dry skin. It sits on top. It does not sink in because there is nothing to sink in with. The result is a visible film on dry skin that takes hours to absorb.

The fix: apply to damp skin immediately after washing. The moisture already on the skin is what the butter integrates into. The butter seals over water, not over dryness.

---

## Amount

Most people use too much.

A small amount, roughly the size of a five-shilling coin, is enough for both arms. The same for both legs. The body requires less than expected when skin is damp.

More is not more effective. It is just more product sitting on the surface.

---

## The application method

Work the butter between your palms first. Body heat melts it from solid to a thin liquid. A few seconds of friction is enough.

Then press and smooth onto the skin rather than rubbing. Rubbing produces friction heat that evaporates some of the moisture you are trying to seal in. Pressing is more effective.

Smooth outward from the centre of each limb. Let it sit for thirty seconds before dressing.

---

## The timing window

There is a practical window after washing when skin is damp. It closes fast. If you wait more than two minutes, skin starts to dry and absorption becomes less effective.

Have the product accessible before stepping into the shower. Open the lid, have a towel within reach. The sequence is: wash, pat lightly but not completely dry, apply immediately.

---

## If skin still feels heavy

Check the product composition. Butters high in beeswax or paraffin feel heavier than those using pure shea and plant oils. The occlusive compounds are not equal.

Lovi Skin Butter uses shea, coconut oil, and olive oil. No wax fillers. It absorbs differently from a wax-heavy formulation.

Also check whether you are applying SPF over it in the morning. Some SPF formulations react with butter residue and create a white-cast film. Apply SPF first and let it dry, or use butter at night and SPF as a separate morning step.

---

## Night vs morning use

Night application is optimal for most people. The skin is not exposed to dust, sweat, or UV during sleep. The butter can work without interruption for seven to eight hours.

Morning use works well for legs and arms where skin is less likely to interact with SPF. Avoid face application in the morning if you use SPF.

Consistency matters more than timing. Once daily is enough for most skin types. The barrier rebuilds slowly. Daily input over two to three weeks produces a measurable difference in baseline moisture levels.
