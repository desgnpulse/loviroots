Your skin barrier is a wall. Not a metaphor — an actual physical structure called the stratum corneum, made of dead skin cells held together by lipids. When that wall has gaps, moisture escapes. That is why your skin feels tight in Nairobi at 8am even after you showered.

Most moisturisers fill those gaps temporarily. They sit on the surface, hold water in for a few hours, and then wash off or evaporate.

Unrefined shea butter does something different.

---

Shea butter is approximately 60% fat by weight. A large portion of that is a group of fatty acids called karite acids — found almost nowhere else in nature. These are not just slippery fats that coat the skin. They are structurally similar to the lipids your skin already produces.

That similarity is the reason shea butter does not just sit on top.

The skin recognises these fatty acids as compatible. They integrate into the barrier structure rather than forming a separate layer over it. The result is a barrier that is denser and less porous — one that holds water across the day, not just after application.

---

This is why refined shea butter does less.

Refining shea removes colour, odour, and texture. It also removes a significant portion of the karite acids and the unsaponifiable fraction — the part responsible for anti-inflammatory activity. What remains is a stable, odourless fat with a fraction of the active compounds.

Unrefined shea is yellow or grey-white. It smells faintly earthy. It is less shelf-stable. Every one of those "flaws" is evidence that the active compounds are still in it.

---

If your skin is chronically dry — tight after washing, flaky in the dry season, rough at the elbows — the issue is almost certainly barrier function, not hydration.

Drinking more water will not fix a leaky barrier. Neither will a lightweight gel moisturiser applied twice a day.

What works is consistent application of something that can rebuild what the barrier is missing. Unrefined shea butter, applied after washing while the skin is still slightly damp, does exactly that.

---

Give it three weeks before judging. The barrier does not rebuild overnight.

That is why customer results with Lovi Shea Butter consistently show up around day 21. Not because the product finally "kicks in" — because that is how long it takes the skin to replace enough of its outer layer with the new lipid structure.

The results are not dramatic. They are steady. Skin that used to need moisturiser every few hours stops needing it. Elbows that cracked every dry season stay intact.

That is barrier repair. It is not glamorous. It is what works.
